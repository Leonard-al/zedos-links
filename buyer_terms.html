🔒 Your money is held securely in escrow and is only released to the seller once you confirm that you have received your order in good condition. You are always in control of when the seller gets paid.

6. Confirming Receipt, Disputes & Fund Release Important

How the payment release process works, step by step:

1
You place an order and pay through the Zedos in-app payment system. Your funds are held securely in escrow — the seller does not receive the money yet.
2
Your order is delivered. You have 48 hours from the time of delivery to either confirm receipt or open a dispute if there is a problem with your order.
3
If you are satisfied, tap "Confirm Receipt" in the app. Your funds are then released to the seller immediately. Once released, the payment is final and cannot be refunded.
4
If there is a problem — wrong item, damaged goods, item not as described — do not confirm receipt. Instead, open a dispute within 48 hours of receiving the item so Zedos can review the case before any funds are released.
5
If you neither confirm receipt nor open a dispute within 48 hours, Zedos will assume you are satisfied with your order and the funds will be automatically released to the seller.

⚠️ Once Funds Are Released — No Refunds

Funds released to the seller are final. Once you confirm receipt of your order, or once the 48-hour window closes without a dispute, the payment is permanently transferred to the seller and Zedos cannot issue a refund.

If you have any issue with your order — damaged item, wrong product, item not as described — you must open a dispute within 48 hours of receiving the item, before confirming receipt and before the release window closes. Disputes raised after funds have been released will not be eligible for a refund.

Do not confirm receipt until you are completely satisfied with your order.

⏱️ 48-Hour Rule — Do Not Miss This Window

If you receive your order and have a problem with it, you have exactly 48 hours to open a dispute in the app. After this window, Zedos will treat your order as completed and satisfactorily received, and funds will be released to the seller with no possibility of a refund through Zedos.

7. Cancellations and Refunds

  • If you paid via the Zedos in-app payment system and your order is cancelled before delivery, your held payment will be refunded to your original payment method.
  • If an item arrives damaged, incorrect, or not as described, do not confirm receipt — raise a dispute within 48 hours of delivery. Zedos will review the case and the held payment may be refunded at Zedos' discretion.
  • Once you confirm receipt of an order, or once the 48-hour window closes, funds are released and no refund can be processed. Always inspect your order carefully before confirming.
  • Cancellations and refunds for orders paid outside the platform are handled directly between buyer and seller; Zedos does not guarantee such refunds but may assist in unresolved disputes.

8. Disputes and Complaints

  • Disputes relating to in-app payments must be raised within 48 hours of receiving the item — and before confirming receipt — to be eligible for Zedos mediation and a potential refund.
  • Disputes raised after funds have already been released to the seller are not eligible for a Zedos-mediated refund.
