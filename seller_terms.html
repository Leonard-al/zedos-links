4. Transactions and Payment Flow Updated

💰 In-app payments are now live on Zedos. When a buyer pays through the Zedos in-app system, the funds are held securely in escrow by Zedos. They will appear in your earnings balance immediately, but they are not withdrawable until the buyer confirms receipt of their order — or until the 48-hour release window closes.

✅ This system protects both you and the buyer, and ensures trust across the Zedos marketplace.

Here is exactly how the payment flow works from your side as a seller:

1
A buyer places an order and pays through the Zedos in-app payment system. The payment is held in escrow by Zedos. You will see the amount reflected in your earnings, but it is marked as pending — you cannot withdraw it yet.
2
You prepare and deliver the order. Once delivered, the buyer has 48 hours to either confirm receipt or open a dispute.
3
If the buyer confirms receipt within 48 hours, the funds are immediately released and become withdrawable. The order is complete.
4
If the buyer does not confirm receipt and does not open a dispute within 48 hours, Zedos will automatically release the funds to you. The order will be treated as completed and satisfactorily received.
5
Once funds are released — whether by buyer confirmation or automatic release after 48 hours — the buyer can no longer request a refund or open a dispute. The payment is yours, final and unconditional.

✅ Funds become withdrawable when:

The buyer confirms receipt of their order, OR 48 hours pass after delivery without a confirmation or dispute.

🔒 Funds remain on hold while:

The order is pending delivery, or the buyer is within their 48-hour confirmation window, or an active dispute is under review.

5. Buyer Disputes — Your Obligations Important

If a buyer raises a dispute within their 48-hour window (for example, an item was damaged, incorrect, or not as described), you as the seller are required to respond promptly.

1
The buyer opens a dispute within 48 hours of receiving the order. You will be notified immediately through the app.
2
You have 3 days (72 hours) from the time the dispute is raised to respond and engage with the buyer or Zedos to resolve the matter.
3
If you do not respond within 3 days, Zedos will treat your silence as non-cooperation. Zedos will automatically close the order and issue a full refund to the buyer from the escrowed funds.
4
Once a refund has been automatically issued due to your non-response, you forfeit the right to contest or appeal that decision. No complaint regarding that transaction will be considered by Zedos.

⚠️ Respond to Disputes Within 3 Days — No Exceptions

Failure to respond to a buyer dispute within 3 days will result in an automatic refund to the buyer and permanent closure of that order. You will not be able to raise a complaint or appeal after a refund has been automatically processed due to non-response.

Always monitor your notifications and respond to disputes promptly. Unresolved disputes damage your seller score and may lead to account suspension.

⏱️ Key Timeframes to Remember

48 hours — the buyer's window to confirm receipt or open a dispute after delivery. After this, funds are automatically released to you and no refund can be requested.

3 days — your window to respond to an open buyer dispute. After this, Zedos will automatically close the order and refund the buyer at your expense, with no right of appeal.
